# Brawler Class - Void Playtest v1.5

## Basic Information
- **Class**: Brawler  
- **Description**: A martial artist/unarmed combat specialist
- **Domains**: Bone & Valor
- **Starting Evasion**: 10 (I can see "Start at 10" under Evasion)
- **Starting Hit Points**: 6
- **Playtest Status**: Void - Playtest v1.5

## Hope Feature
- **Name**: Staggering Strike
- **Cost**: 3 Hope
- **Effect**: When you succeed on an attack, temporarily Stagger your target and force them to mark a Stress. While Staggered, they have disadvantage on attack rolls.

## Class Features

### I Am the Weapon
While you don't have any equipped weapons:
- You gain a +1 bonus to Evasion
- Your unarmed strikes are considered a Melee weapon, use the trait of your choice, and deal **d8+6 phy damage** using your Proficiency

### Combo Strikes
- **Combo Die Options**: d4, d6, d8, d10
- **Mechanic**: After making a damage roll with a Melee weapon but before dealing that damage to the target, mark a Stress to start a combo strike. When you do, roll your Combo Die and add that value. Then, roll your Combo Die again. If the value of the second roll is equal to or greater than your first roll, continue rolling until the last Combo Die's roll is less than the roll that preceded it. Total all rolled values and add that amount to your weapon's damage.
- **Progression**: Your Combo Die starts as a d4. When you level up, once per tier you may use one of your advancement options to increase your Combo Die instead.

## Subclasses

The Brawler has two distinct subclasses with different approaches to combat:

### Juggernaut
**Focus**: Raw power and multi-target destruction

#### Foundation Features
- **Powerhouse**: Increase the d8 damage dice for your unarmed attack to d10. Additionally, you can mark a Stress to target two creatures within Melee range with a single attack roll.

#### Specialization Features  
- **Rugged**: Gain a permanent +3 bonus to your Severe damage threshold. Additionally, your "Powerhouse" feature can target up to three creatures instead of two.

#### Mastery Features
- **Pummeljoy**: When you achieve a critical success on a weapon attack in Melee range, you gain an additional Hope, clear an additional Stress, and gain a +1 bonus to your Proficiency for the attack.
- **Not Done Yet**: When you would mark more than one Hit Point from an attack, you can gain a Hope or clear a Stress.

### Martial Artist  
**Focus**: Stance-based combat with Focus resource management

#### Foundation Features
- **Martial Form**: Take the Martial Form sheet (see below). You start with two martial stances from Tier 1. When you reach a new tier, take two martial stances of your tier or lower.
- **Focus Mechanics**: When you rest, roll a number of d6s equal to your Instinct and place a number of Focus tokens equal to the highest value rolled on this card. Spend a Focus to shift into a stance until you take Severe damage, the scene ends, you mark your last Hit Point, or you shift into another stance.

#### Specialization Features
- **Keen Defenses**: When you are targeted by an attack, you can spend a Focus to give the attack roll disadvantage.
- **Spirit Blast**: Spend a Focus to make an Instinct Roll against an adversary within Close range. On a success, deal d20+3 magic damage using your Proficiency, and you can spend an additional Focus to make them temporarily Vulnerable.

#### Mastery Features
- **Limit Breaker**: Once per rest, you can perform an unbelievable feat such as running across water, leaping between distant rooftops, or scaling the side of a building without needing to roll. When you do, gain a Hope and clear a Stress.

## Martial Form System (Martial Artist Only)

### Stance Progression by Tier

#### Tier 1 Stances
- **Brute**: When you roll the maximum value on a damage die, roll an additional damage die
- **Defensive**: Attack rolls against you have disadvantage unless the adversary marks a Stress
- **Grappling**: On a successful attack, you can spend a Focus to make the target temporarily Restrained
- **Steady**: Gain a -1 penalty to your Evasion. When you deal damage on a successful attack, add an additional damage die and drop the lowest
- **Precise**: Gain a +1 bonus to your attack rolls
- **Quick**: When making an attack roll, spend a Focus to include an additional target within range

#### Tier 2 Stances
- **Deadly**: When you deal Severe damage, the target marks an additional HP
- **Hindering**: On a successful attack, you can spend a Focus to make the target temporarily Hindered. While Hindered, their attack rolls have a -2 penalty
- **Invigorating**: When you make a successful attack, roll a d4. On a 4, gain a Focus
- **Immovable**: Gain a +2 bonus to your damage thresholds and you cannot be moved unwillingly
- **Nimble**: When an adversary makes a successful attack roll against you, you can spend a number of Focus up to your Tier. Roll a number of d6s equal to the Focus spent. If any of the dice results match, you take no damage from the attack
- **Otherworldly**: You can choose whether to do Physical or Magic damage
- **Scary**: Successful attacks also force the target to mark a Stress

#### Tier 3 Stances
- **Deflecting**: When you are targeted by an attack, you can spend 2 Focus to gain a bonus to your Evasion equal to your Armor Score against the attack
- **Devastating**: Spend a Focus before your attack roll to use d20s as your damage dice instead
- **Dueling**: Gain advantage on attack rolls when there are no other adversaries or allies in Very Close range of you or your target
- **Compounding**: When you roll the maximum value on a Combo Die, increase the size of all subsequent Combo Dice you roll for the attack (at a maximum of d12s)
- **Inexorable**: Attacks against you have disadvantage if you have more than two adversaries within Melee of you
- **Favored**: Add the trait of your choice to your damage roll
- **Sheltering**: When you mark an Armor Slot, it reduces damage for you and all allies within Melee range of you who took the same damage

#### Tier 4 Stances
- **Crushing**: When you roll on a damage die, it can be treated as the highest value on the die instead
- **Infuriating**: On a successful attack, the target becomes Furious. While Furious, they are Vulnerable and have a -2 penalty on attack rolls. They can only clear Furious by succeeding on an attack against you
- **Sweeping**: Your attack targets all adversaries within Very Close range. Deal half damage to all targets you succeed against
- **Discerning**: You may choose the target of your attack after making your attack roll. The GM will tell you who the attack would succeed against

## Key Mechanics Analysis
- **Dual Subclass Paths**: Choose between raw power (Juggernaut) or tactical flexibility (Martial Artist)
- **Unarmed Combat Focus**: Powerful when fighting without weapons (d8+6 base, d10+6 for Juggernaut)
- **Combo System**: Risk/reward mechanic using Stress to potentially deal massive damage
- **Multi-Target Combat**: Both subclasses can attack multiple enemies
- **Resource Management**: Focus system for Martial Artist, Stress management for Juggernaut
- **Bone & Valor Domains**: Access to death/fear magic and courage/tactics magic

### Juggernaut Highlights
- **Raw Power**: d10 unarmed attacks, +3 Severe damage threshold
- **Area Control**: Attack up to 3 creatures simultaneously
- **Survivability**: "Not Done Yet" provides Hope/Stress benefits when taking heavy damage

### Martial Artist Highlights  
- **Stance Flexibility**: 16 different martial stances across 4 tiers
- **Focus System**: Instinct-based resource generation (roll d6s = Instinct, take highest)
- **Magical Options**: Spirit Blast (d20+3 magic damage) and "Limit Breaker" supernatural feats

## Tier Progression System
The Brawler has a unique advancement system focused on:
- **Proficiency Bonuses**: Gain +1 to Proficiency at multiple tiers
- **Trait Bonuses**: +1 bonus to two unmarked character traits
- **Physical Stats**: Additional Hit Points and Stress slots
- **Experience Bonuses**: +2 bonus to Experiences
- **Domain Cards**: Additional domain cards from accessible domains
- **Evasion Bonus**: +1 bonus to Evasion

## Analysis Notes
- **Unarmed Specialist**: Designed to fight without weapons but can use them
- **High Instinct Focus**: Primary suggested trait (+2) - critical for Focus generation in Martial Artist
- **Balanced Physical Stats**: Good mix of Agility and Strength for mobility and power
- **Risk/Reward Combat**: Combo system rewards aggressive play but costs Stress
- **Subclass Synergy**: Both subclasses work well with the base Combo Strike system
- **Versatile Advancement**: Multiple paths for character growth through stances or raw power
